Recap-claim

The National Institute of Standards and Technology are trying to solve the problem of racial bias in facial recognition to avoid arresting innocent people

Explanation

It has been proven how harmful false positive facial scans have been for many people of color. In this case Robert Willaims was misidentified by a facial recognition program implemented by the Detroit Police department, which led to his wrongful detainment.

Most Effective-claim

Proper Representation in data sets should be used to mitigate any existing bias

Explanation

In this case studied by the National Institute of Standards and Technology learned that Robert Williams was at a greater risk of being misidentified, because he was a black man. On the other hand the opposite was true for white people. They believe that this is caused by “limited diversity in training images” which cause algorithms to be unable to distinguish unique facial features of darker skin tones.

Use of Technology-claim

Luxand is a biometric algorithm used by police forces mainly in Europe and is very accurate compared to ones used in America.

Explanation

This is an extremely highly effective Algorithm that is able to properly recognize facial features (up to 66) compared to many used in America. The company develops and markets a complete set of tools, libraries and solutions to perform fully automatic recognition of human faces and facial features. This means that the problems stated above are less of an issue.

Implications

The best way to fix this systemic problem is to switch over to the balanced data sets that companies such as Luxand use and account for all races and features. Biometrics should also not be used in every situation and be limited to severe instances when they are the only option.
